## Rotation guide: how to pilot this build in real fights

The biggest mistake with high-damage lists is burning SP too early. You want a rhythm: setup, pressure, then burst at the right time.

### Standard opener (safe pressure)

1. Apply **Infect** to your highest-value target.
2. Use **Sword Toss** if positioning/flying enemies demand range.
3. Save SP for Charge turns instead of overcommitting early.
4. Use **Daze** when an enemy’s next action is threatening.

### Burst pattern (delete priority target)

1. Stack **Charge** (single or double depending on SP and threat).
2. Use **Sword Toss** (or your best available sword attack) into the highest danger target.
3. Follow with poison upkeep so pressure continues after the burst.

### Defensive stabilization turns

1. Keep **Defend Plus** value active through disciplined guarding.
2. Use **Thorns** only if your guard timing is reliable.
3. Summon **Brad Daniel** at moments where incoming multi-hit pressure spikes.

| Situation | Best Action | SP Priority | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Single dangerous enemy** | Charge into focused burst | **High** | Remove threat immediately |
| **Two-front pressure** | Daze front + poison uptime | **Medium** | Reduce incoming turns |
| **Flying/backline threat** | Sword Toss target swap | **Low-Med** | Prevent stalled turns |
| **Sustained boss phase** | Bodyguard support + steady poison | **High on support timing** | Survive while scaling damage |

> **💡 Tip:** If you can’t secure a clean burst KO, it’s often better to refresh poison/control than to force a weak all-in turn.

## Defensive tech that keeps Aggressor playable

Aggressor’s downside is real, so the defensive side is not optional. You’re building a high-damage list, not a glass cannon that collapses in longer fights.

### Why Defend Plus matters
A permanent point of base defense smooths every enemy turn. Over a full encounter, that often prevents more damage than one-time healing.

### Why Thorns is high skill, high value
Thorns rewards precise guarding with extra passive punishment. If your timing is off, the tighter guard window can hurt more than help, so treat it as a mastery card.

### Why Call Griefer is the stabilizer
Brad Daniel’s bodyguard effect can redirect a large share of incoming pressure, which is exactly what this archetype needs during dangerous boss patterns. It also adds extra utility through poison-capable damage options.

| Defensive Tool | Strength | Limitation | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Defend Plus** | Reliable flat mitigation | No burst utility | Any long fight |
| **Thorns** | Passive retaliation on successful guard | Smaller guard window | Skilled defensive players |
| **Call Griefer** | Bodyguard + utility actions | SP cost, summon limits | Boss spikes, tough waves |

If you feel the **Block Tales best build** is too risky, the issue is usually not damage—it’s underinvesting in these mitigation layers.
