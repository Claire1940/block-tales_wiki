3. End session with one skill objective (like Good Noodle), not five.

This pacing keeps your **block tales achievements** progress steady and avoids mental fatigue from repetitive clicking tasks.

## A-Tier Skill Checks: Combat Mastery and Build Discipline

A-tier is where your build and timing matter more than persistence. You’ll want stronger fundamentals in guarding, turn planning, and buff stacking.

| A-Tier Achievement | What It Tests | Build/Strategy Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **True DPS** | Burst damage setup | Stack additive buffs and attack-order bonuses |
| **Blocker** | Boss pattern defense | Perfect guard timing under stricter windows |
| **All Cards milestone** | Economy and collection depth | Tick budgeting + selective farming |
| **Redemption** | Demo 3 boss execution | Anti-confusion prep and targeted healing |
| **Heat Resistant** | AoE adaptation | Party spacing logic and survival rotation |

### True DPS shortcut logic
To hit high burst thresholds, combine:
- Base attack that scales well with buffs
- At least one direct damage increase card
- Situational bonus from action order/opening hit
- Temporary food/item amplifier before damage turn

### Economy benchmark for card hunters
Plan for a large tick sink if buying many cards outright. If you’re grinding drops, target enemies with overlapping loot tables so each farm loop supports multiple **Block Tales achievements** at once.

> **Tip:** If a boss attempt fails, review turns—not just gear. Most A-tier clears improve faster from better sequencing than from raw stat upgrades.

## S-Tier Wall: RNG, Super Bosses, and High-Pressure Clears

These are the hardest **block tales achievements** because they combine skill and variance. Don’t attempt them back-to-back without recovery sessions.

| S-Tier Achievement | Main Pain Point | Best Preparation |
|---|---|---|
| **King of the Sea** | Very low rare fish rates | Set a hard tick budget per session |
| **Is This a Joke** | Multi-layer RNG frog scenario | Patrol spawn cycles; avoid overcommitting |
| **Alternative Reality** | Lifesteal + piercing boss pressure | Heavy sustain items and summon control |
| **Monster Hunter** | Hard-mode multi-boss gauntlet | Defensive loadouts and HP padding |
| **Emerging Timelines** | Super boss burst/summon threat | Save energy/resources for add-clearing windows |

### Three practical S-tier stabilizers
1. **Session caps for RNG goals**  
   Example: Stop fishing after X worms/ticks. This protects morale and economy.

2. **Summon-first target rules**  
   Against healing or high-damage adds, eliminate support mobs before boss race attempts.

3. **Defensive pivot loadout**  
   For hard mode, sacrifice some DPS for survivability if incoming AoE can erase party tempo.

> **Warning:** Grinding while tilted ruins decision quality. If your reaction timing drops, switch to low-pressure collection tasks and return later.

## FAQ

**Q: What’s the best order to complete block tales achievements in 2026?**

A: Start with F/D easy unlocks, then clear C/B grind objectives, move into A-tier combat checks, and save S-tier RNG and super-boss goals for last. This order protects your resources and reduces burnout.

**Q: Which block tales achievements are hardest because of RNG, not skill?**

A: King of the Sea and Is This a Joke are prime examples. They can consume major time even with perfect planning, so use strict session limits and tick budgets.

**Q: How many ticks should I save before serious card and item achievement pushes?**

A: Keep a reserve large enough to fund both purchases and recovery attempts. For heavy collection milestones, plan a multi-thousand tick budget and avoid impulse spending between runs.

**Q: Is solo play viable for high-tier block tales achievements?**

A: Yes for many goals, but coordinated runs (or controlled alt setups) make certain objectives easier—especially rescue-style achievements and hard-mode boss consistency checks.
