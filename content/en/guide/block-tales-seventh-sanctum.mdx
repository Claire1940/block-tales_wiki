## Room-by-Room Route Strategy (Fast but Safe)

Most failures happen in transitions, not the toughest boss pattern itself. Your goal is to avoid taking “free damage” between major encounters.

### Phase 1: Entry rooms
- Pull only line-of-sight safe packs
- Keep enemies in one cone/arc
- Use low-cooldown tools instead of major burst

### Phase 2: Mid sanctum pressure
- Prioritize ranged interrupters first
- Rotate defensive cooldowns instead of stacking all at once
- Reposition before your resource bar is empty

### Phase 3: Final pressure window
- Save one emergency defensive
- Call targets clearly
- Avoid tunnel vision when boss/add overlap begins

| Route Segment | Clear Goal | Recommended Tempo | Reset Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | Zero avoidable damage | Controlled, medium speed | Two players forced to burn major defensives early |
| **Mid rooms** | Preserve resources | Intentional pulls, no overchain | Team split positioning + missed interrupt wave |
| **Pre-final** | Full cooldown alignment | Brief pause, then commit | No defensive tools available for next overlap |
| **Final push** | Clean execution | Fast only after safe setup | Repeated knockbacks breaking formation |

> **Warning:** If your team is already unstable by mid sanctum, pushing faster usually makes recovery harder. Pause 10–15 seconds, realign cooldowns, then continue.

## Combat Rhythm: Cooldown Rotation and Mistake Recovery

A practical way to improve **block tales seventh sanctum** performance is to think in “cycles” instead of isolated buttons:

1. **Open cycle:** establish control (group enemies, apply debuffs)
2. **Pressure cycle:** sustained damage + selective burst
3. **Stabilize cycle:** defensive refresh, quick reposition, resource top-up

Many teams fail because everyone enters pressure cycle at the same moment and has nothing left for stabilize cycle. Stagger your power spikes.

### Basic rotation model (adapt to your class)

- Start with control/utility skill
- Use 1–2 mid cooldown damage skills
- Hold top burst for priority enemy or exposed window
- Reposition before stamina/resource collapse
- Re-engage with short cooldowns while long cooldowns recover

If a mistake happens:
- Stop greed damage immediately
- Re-establish enemy angle
- Spend one defensive, not all defensives
- Resume normal cycle after stability returns

This approach is especially useful in repeated **block tales seventh sanctum** runs where consistency matters more than one lucky speed clear.
