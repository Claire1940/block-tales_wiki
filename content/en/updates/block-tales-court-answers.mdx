## The Chapter 2 Trial Overview

The trial in Block Tales serves as a narrative climax where your actions in previous areas are scrutinized. Unlike standard turn-based battles, the "battle" here is one of wits and words. Choosing the wrong response can lead to harsher combat modifiers or the loss of specific items. The court is presided over by rigorous NPCs who will not hesitate to penalize you for inconsistencies in your testimony.

To prepare for the trial, you should review the events occurring in the Manor and the interactions with the townspeople of Bisville. Most of the questions revolve around the timeline of your arrival and the specific items you interacted with during your exploration.

### The Core Cards of the Juggernaut

The foundation of this build relies on the **HP Drain** card. By equipping two of these, you heal 2 HP every time you land a successful hit. While this does impose a -2 damage penalty, the sheer survivability it provides is unmatched. You can acquire these cards from Amber and Telmon’s Manor for 85 ticks or from the Sketchy Figure in Bisville for two bucks each.

To complement the healing, you need the **Resurrect** card. At a cost of 3 SP (reduced to 2 with SP Saver), it allows you to revive teammates. More importantly, if you have enough SP when you are downed, you gain a one-time self-revive per battle, skipping two enemy turns in solo play.

## Advanced Combat Tactics for Chapter 2

Once you have mastered the **block tales court answers**, you must master the Action Commands. The Juggernaut build utilizes cards like **Anti-up** and **Power Stab**, which significantly decrease your timing windows. If you miss these hits, you deal zero damage and receive zero healing from your HP Drain cards.

1.  **Practice on Weak Enemies:** Before heading into the boss fights following the court scene, spend time in Bisville Meadows to get the rhythm of the smaller timing windows.
2.  **Manage Your SP:** Always keep at least 2 SP in reserve. This ensures that if you are knocked down, your **Resurrect** card can trigger the self-revive mechanic.
3.  **Utilize Status Effects:** The **Days** card is incredibly efficient. For just 1 SP (with SP Saver), it applies Dizzy Level 1 for four turns. Even if you miss the timing, the debuff still applies, making it a safe choice during high-pressure turns.

> **Warning:** The **Aggressor** card causes you to take "instant damage," meaning your HP doesn't roll down slowly. This can be jarring if you are used to the standard damage mechanic, so ensure your Defense is high enough to mitigate the initial impact.

## Card Synergy and Damage Calculation

To understand why this build is recommended alongside the **block tales court answers** for late-game progression, look at the math. By stacking **Knight**, **Aggressor**, **Anti-up**, and **Power Stab**, you gain a +4 damage bonus. Even with the -2 penalty from double **HP Drain**, you are still hitting for +2 over your base damage while healing 2 HP per hit.

If you add the **Deep Focus** cards (stacking up to three), you generate 30% more energy when using the Focus command. This allows you to spam high-cost moves like **Sword Toss** (to hit flying enemies) or **Pity SP** (trading HP for SP) without running dry.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I get the block tales court answers wrong?**

A: Providing incorrect answers typically leads to a "Guilty" verdict or a harsher sentencing. This usually results in a mandatory combat encounter with added debuffs or the loss of a small amount of Ticks/currency.

**Q: Where can I find the Sketchy Figure to buy the necessary cards?**

A: The Sketchy Figure is located in an alleyway in Bisville. He sells high-tier cards like Anti-up, SP Saver, and HP Drain for "bucks," which is a secondary currency found throughout Chapter 2.

**Q: Is the Juggernaut build viable for the entire game?**

A: While extremely powerful in Chapter 2 and Chapter 3, the build relies heavily on sword attacks. If you encounter enemies with high physical resistance or mechanics that punish healing, you may need to swap a few cards for magical or ball-based alternatives.

**Q: Can I redo the trial if I fail to give the right block tales court answers?**

A: Most story-critical trials in Block Tales will allow you to retry from the last save point if you fail. However, some dialogue choices have permanent minor effects on NPC relationships, so it is best to get them right the first time.
