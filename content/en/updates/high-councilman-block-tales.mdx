## Phase-by-Phase Strategy for Consistent Clears

Treat the encounter as a loop of mini-goals. Don’t think “win the whole fight.” Think “solve the next 10 seconds.”

### Phase 1: Pattern identification and safe opening

Early phase usually tests your patience. Learn the timing of the first repeated sequence and only punish after confirmed recovery frames.

| Phase 1 Goal | Action | Success Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Identify opener** | Watch first 2-3 attack cycles | You can predict movement checks |
| **Limit greed** | Use short combos only | HP remains stable above danger line |
| **Track cooldowns** | Save one defensive skill | You can answer surprise pressure |

### Phase 2: Pressure and disruption

This is where many **high councilman block tales** attempts collapse. Mechanics come faster, and panic inputs stack mistakes.

| Phase 2 Problem | Correct Response | What to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Back-to-back pressure** | Reset spacing, then re-engage | Trading hits “to keep DPS up” |
| **Stamina/resource dip** | Use micro-downtime to recover | Emptying bars during unsafe windows |
| **Team desync** | Call cooldown order out loud | Random burst overlap |

### Phase 3: Closing discipline

When boss HP gets low, players rush and wipe. You win by preserving structure.

1. **Keep the same defensive rhythm** from earlier phases.  
2. **Use burst only on confirmed punish windows.**  
3. **Commit to one final rotation plan** instead of improvising five different ones.  

> **⚠️ Warning:** The last 15–20% of HP is where “hero plays” fail most often. If you reached this point cleanly, disciplined damage is the fastest finish.

## Most Common Mistakes (and How to Fix Them Fast)

Players usually don’t fail from one giant error. They fail from repeating small errors every 20 seconds.

### Mistake 1: Entering with a speedrun mindset

You can clear faster by being cleaner, not by attacking nonstop.

**Fix:** Cap yourself to short combo strings until you can complete three consecutive dodge cycles without taking heavy damage.

### Mistake 2: Misusing emergency tools

Burning sustain early leaves no answer for real danger.

**Fix:** Define a trigger rule before pull (example: “Use emergency heal only below critical threshold after unavoidable overlap”).

### Mistake 3: Bad camera and arena awareness

Losing sight of telegraphs causes avoidable chain hits.

**Fix:** Raise camera sensitivity slightly, zoom to a mid distance, and avoid over-rotating during close-range combos.

### Mistake 4: Team comms too vague

“Careful” is not a callout.

**Fix:** Use short, specific calls: “Burst now,” “Hold cooldown,” “Reset left,” “Revive after wave.”

> **💡 Tip:** Record one failed run and review only your deaths. In most **high councilman block tales** wipes, one repeated habit is obvious after a replay.
