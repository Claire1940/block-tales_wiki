## Phase-by-Phase Strategy for Opticus

Treat each phase as a separate mini-fight with its own win condition.

### Phase 1: Information Phase (Learn and Stabilize)
Goal: avoid unnecessary damage and map rhythm cues.

- Stay disciplined with spacing.
- Take “safe damage” only; don’t force greed windows.
- Watch how your arena lanes shift and where pressure repeats.

**Win condition:** enter next phase with cooldowns mostly intact and minimal downs.

### Phase 2: Pressure Phase (Control and Rotate)
Goal: survive overlap mechanics while maintaining medium DPS.

- Rotate as a unit when lane pressure escalates.
- Avoid crossing through teammates during hazard ticks.
- Use one planned defensive tool—not all of them.

**Win condition:** no panic revives, no full-resource dump.

### Phase 3: Conversion Phase (Close Cleanly)
Goal: convert resources into controlled kill pressure.

- Use stored burst in pre-defined windows.
- If someone drops, only recover on a known safe cycle.
- Keep callouts short: “reset,” “rotate right,” “hold burst,” “go.”

**Win condition:** finish with structure, not desperation.

## Arena Positioning and Movement Discipline

Most failed clears come from poor footwork. Here’s the simple positioning model:

- Divide arena into **three logical lanes**.
- Assign each player a default lane and one fallback lane.
- Rotate clockwise (or counterclockwise) as a team—never split decisions.

### Movement rules to enforce
1. **No diagonal panic dashes** through the center during pressure spikes.  
2. **No double reposition** unless caller confirms.  
3. **Reset after every burst** to default lane.  

This structure prevents collision damage, keeps support ranges stable, and makes revive opportunities readable.

### Solo vs team note
In solo runs, your challenge is stamina and patience. In team runs, your challenge is discipline and communication. For **block tales demo 5 opticus**, team wipes are often “good players making different correct decisions at the same time.” Standardize decisions before the pull.

## Common Mistakes in block tales demo 5 opticus (and Fixes)

### Mistake 1: Overheating early
Players overuse resources in Phase 1 and arrive empty for Phase 3.

**Fix:** cap opener spend to a pre-agreed limit.

### Mistake 2: Greedy revive attempts
A downed teammate pulls others into hazard cycles.

**Fix:** revive only on caller confirmation.

### Mistake 3: Unclear callouts
Long, emotional callouts create delay and confusion.

**Fix:** use fixed keywords:
- “Hold”
- “Rotate”
- “Reset”
- “Go”

### Mistake 4: Chasing lost damage
After a bad cycle, players rush to “make up DPS,” causing chain errors.

**Fix:** recover first. You win this fight by consistency.
