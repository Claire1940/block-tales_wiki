## Prerequisites for the Griefer Card Quest

Before you can begin the journey to cure the Griefer and earn your reward, you must meet specific progression milestones. The quest is tied to the content released in Demo 4, meaning newer players will need to progress through the main story before this option becomes available.

| Requirement Type | Specific Milestone | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Story Progression** | Complete Demo 4 | Unlocks the necessary world states and NPCs. |
| **Key Item** | Battleship Card | Required to access the hidden Cookbook area. |
| **Location Access** | Demo 2 Village & Row Town | Locations for gathering ingredients and quest triggers. |

> **Note:** Do not attempt this quest until you have officially unlocked the Battleship card (sometimes referred to as the "bottled chip"), as it is the only way to interact with the environment needed to find the quest-critical Cookbook.

## Step 1: Initiating the Quest with the Mayor

Once you have met the prerequisites, your first stop is the Demo 2 Village. Teleport to this location and look for the Mayor. He can typically be found near the jeep. Engaging in dialogue with the Mayor sets the narrative in motion, as he expresses the need to help a "Griefer" who requires a specific cure.

After speaking with the Mayor, enter the jeep. This vehicle is essential for traversing the terrain to find the secret area where the Cookbook is hidden. The path is specific, so ensure you are familiar with the Demo 2 outskirts.

## Step 2: Locating the Cookbook

Drive the jeep toward the designated cliffside or clearing (follow the pathing shown in the exploration phase of Demo 4). At this location, you will need to utilize your Battleship card. Using this card triggers a world interaction that reveals or allows you to reach the Cookbook.

| Item | Location | Tool Needed |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cookbook** | Secret Area (Demo 2 Outskirts) | **Battleship Card** |
| **Onion** | Row Town | General Exploration |
| **Apple Core** | Demo 2 Village | General Exploration |

Collecting the Cookbook is the "point of no return" for the quest. Once you have it, you are officially on the path to becoming a chef capable of brewing the "Special" concoction.

## Step 3: Gathering the Ingredients

With the Cookbook in your possession, you now need the raw materials. You will need to travel between two main hubs to find what you need.

1.  **Row Town:** Head here to find the **Onion** (or Onion Ring). This is a standard item found in the town’s interactable environments or shops.
2.  **Demo 2 Village:** Return here to find the **Apple Core**. 

Having both items in your inventory is mandatory before visiting the Ghost Chef, as he will not be able to assist you with partial ingredients.

## Step 4: The Ghost Chef and the Cooking Process

The Ghost Chef is the only NPC capable of interpreting the Cookbook you found. Teleport to his kitchen and present the Cookbook to him. This unlocks the advanced cooking menu required for the "Special."

The cooking process is a two-stage craft. You cannot simply throw everything into a pot at once. Follow these specific steps:

### Stage 1: Creating Compost
First, take your **Apple Core** and interact with the Ghost Chef's cooking station. You must process the Apple Core into **Compost**. While this sounds unappetizing, it is a vital base for the cure.

### Stage 2: Brewing "The Special"
Next, combine the newly created **Compost** with the **Onion** you gathered from Row Town. The Ghost Chef will then produce a unique item known as "**The Special**." This item is categorized as a quest-critical consumable and is the key to finishing the quest.

| Input Item A | Input Item B | Resulting Item |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Apple Core** | (None) | **Compost** |
| **Compost** | **Onion** | **The Special** |

## Step 5: Curing the Griefer

With "The Special" in your inventory, teleport back to the Demo 2 Village one last time. Locate the Mayor and the Griefer. Upon speaking with the Mayor, you will have the option to hand over the Special. 

A brief dialogue sequence will follow, depicting the curing of the Griefer. Once the dialogue concludes, the game will automatically reward you with the **Block Tales griefer card** and the **True Panacea badge**. 

> **💡 Tip:** Check your card collection immediately to ensure the "Call Griefer" card is slotted. It has unique BP (Battle Point) requirements that you may need to adjust your build for.

## Strategic Use of the Block Tales Griefer Card

The **Block Tales griefer card** acts as a summon. When used in battle, it calls upon the Griefer to perform a "Summon All" attack. This is particularly effective in encounters with multiple enemies, as it deals spread damage and can apply status effects depending on your current level and stats.

In the 2026 meta, the **Block Tales griefer card** is often paired with high-energy builds. Since summons typically cost a significant amount of SP (Skill Points), players should look for equipment that boosts SP regeneration or reduces the cost of "Call" type cards.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the Block Tales griefer card without finishing Demo 4?**

A: No, the quest relies on NPCs and items that are only accessible once the Demo 4 content has been triggered and the Battleship card has been acquired.

**Q: What happens if I lose "The Special" or use the ingredients for something else?**

A: You can typically find the ingredients again in their respective locations (Row Town and Demo 2 Village). The Ghost Chef will remain available to cook the items as long as you have the Cookbook in your possession.

**Q: Is the Block Tales griefer card permanent?**

A: Yes, once you earn the card and the True Panacea badge, it remains in your collection permanently. You can use it in any subsequent battles or even in future demo releases.

**Q: Does the "Call Griefer" attack hit all enemies or just one?**

A: The Griefer's primary ability is a "Summon All" mechanic, meaning it targets all enemies currently on the field, making it excellent for crowd control.
